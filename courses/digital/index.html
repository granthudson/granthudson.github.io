Digital Test and Product Engineering Course

The Digital Test and Product Engineering course teaches the foundations of test and product engineering for digital circuits, processors and memory. Students are exposed to practices of test design and analysis for general digital circuits, but also have the opportunity to apply practices on the testing of an 8-bit microcontroller in laboratory activities. Working with a microcontroller provides students with experience understanding the design and architecture of a complex digital system. Students must develop the skills needed to interpret the data sheet for complex systems such as microcontrollers, and to use these resources to understand the architecture and operation of the components in order to design an appropriate test.


List of Course Topics

Weeks Segment Topics Covered
1 Introduction to Test and Product Engineering Motivation and role of TPE
Cost-accuracy tradeoff
ATE Test Equipment (source/measurement units, Arbitrary Waveform Generators, Digitizers, Relays)
1.5 Micrcontroller Architecture Microcontroller design and architecture
Instruction set architecture and instruction execution process
Datasheet interpretation
2 Fault Modeling and Simulation Common fault models (stuck-at faults, stuck-open/stuck-short faults, behavioral faults)
Fault equivalency
Fault simulation (parallel, deductive, concurrent, differential)
1 Memory Testing March tests
Typical fault models for memory (coupling, transition, pattern sensitive)
RAM and ROM design
1 Measurement Accuracy Repeatability, reproducibility, correlation, calibration
1 Data Analysis Statistical analysis of data, CP, CPK, GRR, guardbanding for error

List of Labs

Lab Topic Instructional Goals
1 Program Order and ATE Resources Students become familiar with ATE equipment, test board, and programming environment
2 Continuity Students create a function, use analog resources, datalog, run the program on ATE, and critically think about results
3 Power Supply Current Students gain more practice in skills from lab 2
4 Input Offset Voltage Students use a false-summing amp for feedback control and experience the challenges in obtaining high-precision measurements
5 Open Loop Gain Students use a nulling-amp for feedback control and gain more experience in obtaining high-precision measurements
6 Pattern-Based Testing Students control instruments using a test pattern (using clocks) and measure Vos, CMRR, and PSRR in one test
7 AC Gain-Bandwidth Students perform a coherent multi-tone test to measure gain-bandwidth specification
8 to 10 Project: Design test program for new chip Students receive a datasheet for a new part and generate: 1) a test plan, 2) production-level test code, and 3) data analysis (GRR) indicating that the program is ready for production
