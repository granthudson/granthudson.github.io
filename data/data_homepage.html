  • Mixed-Signal Device Interface Board (DIB)

    • The mixed-signal DIB is used to test (i) the TLV5616 DAC (top socket), (ii) the ADS7822 ADC (bottom socket), and (iii) the MCP6541 Comparator (PDIP package) using a daughter card that attaches to the mixed-signal DIB.


    Page 1 shows the socket connections and loads for the DAC and ADC. The DAC socket is dual site, with site 1 at the top of the socket and site 2 at the bottom of the socket.
    Page 2 shows relay controls and filtering capability for the reference voltages for the DAC and ADC. The reference voltages were driven directly with a voltage source (APU4 and 5) for our data.
    Page 3 shows the header connections for the daughter card. Instrument connections are passed from the mixed-signal DIB to the daughter card through these header pins.
    Pages 4 and 5 show the fixed power-supply connections.

  • Comparator Daughter Card Device Interface Board (DIB)

    • The Comparator Daughter Card DIB is used with the Mixed-Signal DIB to test the MCP6541 comparator chip.

    Pages 1 and 2 show the socket connections for site 1 and site 2.
    Page 3-5 show the loading options for the comparator.
