DAC Zero Error (ZSE) and Gain Error (GE)

This test measures the zero-scale error and the gain error of the TLV5616 DAC. The zero-scale error test is performed by serially loading a binary 10 into the DAC and measuring the output voltage. Since the zero-scale error can be positive or negative, a binary 10 is used to place the DAC out of the lower deadband given the worst case zero-scale error. The zero-scale error is then calculated by subtracting 10 times the ideal VLSB from the measured value.

The gain error is measured by serially loading a full-scale input into the DAC and measuring the full-scale output voltage. The gain error is calcualted by subtracting the zero-scale error from the full-scale output voltage and finding the percent error relative to the ideal full-scale voltage.

This data set shows the measurements made on 5 different chips, 10 measurements/chip, for two different DIB sites. Looking through this dataset, students can start to see the effects of repeatability (looking across the 10 measurements/chip), reproducibility (looking across the different DIB sites with the same chip), and process variation (looking across different chips with the same site). For all of these measurements, each result represents an average of 5 measurements using a voltmeter (QMS) placed in the 10V range with an output low-pass filter cut-off frequency of 100kHz.
