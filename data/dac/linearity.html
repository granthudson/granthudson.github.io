DAC DNL and INL Data

This test measures the differential nonlinearity (DNL) and integral nonlinearity (INL) of the TLV5616 DAC.

To perform the DNL and INL calculations, first analog outputs are required for every code from 10 to 4095. Digital codes are serially loaded into the DAC and the analog output is measured using a high precision voltage measurement unit.

The DNL specification is calculated from the measurements by finding the voltage difference between neighboring codes (Voutn, Voutn-1), subtracting a ideal VLSB, and dividing the result by an ideal VLSB to normalize the result and place it in LSB units (see below). Only the worst-case calculated DNL is datalogged.
(Voutn - Voutn-1 - VLSB)/VLSB.

The INL specification is calculated from the measurements by finding a line that approximiates the set of codes calculated using the end points (VOINL).
VINLn = Gainep*Din+Vos

where Gain

The INL is found by calculating the difference between each measurement and the line approximating the codes and normalizing the result by the measured gain.
INLn = (VOn - VOINLn)/Gainep
